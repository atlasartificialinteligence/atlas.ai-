## Memory Rules Worth Preserving

Promote information to durable memory when it:

- has structural value;
- connects to projects, areas, resources, decisions, or identity;
- is likely to matter again;
- improves future judgment, continuity, or execution.

Do not preserve information that:

- is disconnected from the ecosystem;
- has no durable use;
- is only temporary residue;
- adds clutter without improving understanding.

When in doubt:

- capture temporarily first;
- promote later through review;
- archive instead of deleting unless deletion is clearly safe.
